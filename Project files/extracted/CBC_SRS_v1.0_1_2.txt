1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functional and non-
functional requirements for the CBC Digital Learning Platform — a national
educational technology platform designed to fully integrate ICT and Artificial
Intelligence into Uganda's Competence-Based Curriculum (CBC). This document
serves as the formal agreement between project stakeholders and the development
team on what the system shall deliver.
1.2 Scope
The CBC Digital Learning Platform is a web-based application with a mobile-
responsive design that enables secondary school learners (S1–S6) across Uganda
to access curriculum-aligned content, interact with an AI-powered tutor grounded in
domestic Ugandan knowledge, collaborate with peers nationally, and contribute to a
shared knowledge ecosystem. The platform supports teachers as content
contributors and provides MoES/NCDC officials with curriculum management and
national analytics capabilities.
The system is scoped for secondary education at launch, with architecture designed
to scale to primary education in future phases.

2. Overall Description
2.1 Product Perspective
The CBC Digital Learning Platform is a new, standalone national system. It does not
replace existing school management systems but complements them by providing a
learner-facing digital environment that extends education beyond the physical
classroom. The platform integrates an AI layer — functioning similarly to how Grok is
embedded in the X (Twitter) platform — making AI assistance a native part of the
learning experience rather than an external tool.
2.2 Product Context and Problem Domain
Uganda's CBC was introduced to shift education from rote learning to competency
attainment. However, several critical gaps prevent full realisation of this vision:
• Learners have no digital platform to access CBC-aligned content outside
school hours.
• There is no national infrastructure connecting learners across the country for
peer knowledge sharing.
• Existing AI tools (such as ChatGPT or Gemini) operate on foreign knowledge
bases with no Ugandan domestic context, making them unreliable for local
academic content.
• Teachers have no scalable mechanism to support every learner's questions
individually.
• MoES and NCDC have no real-time visibility into national learning patterns
and curriculum effectiveness.
This platform addresses all five gaps within a single integrated system.

2.4 Assumptions and Dependencies
• NCDC will provide curriculum content in a structured digital format for
ingestion into the knowledge base.
• MoES will provide policy guidance on data governance and learner data
privacy compliance.
• Schools will register their learners and teachers through an administrative
onboarding process.
• The platform assumes at least 2G connectivity for online features; offline
reading requires prior content download on a connected session.
• AI model access (via API) is available and budgeted for operational costs.

version:
• Parents — benefit from their child's improved access to learning resources.

• Research Community — benefit from the knowledge sharing and research
contribution ecosystem generated by learners.
• Community — broader Ugandan society benefits from improved digital literacy
and educational outcomes.
