1. Introduction
This document presents the Stage B Use Case Modelling output for the CBC Digital Learning
Platform. It derives from the Software Requirements Specification (SRS v1.0) and transforms all
functional requirements (FR-L, FR-T, FR-M, FR-AI) into structured UML Use Case Diagrams and
formal use case descriptions.
The use cases define every meaningful interaction between the system's actors and the platform,
establishing the behavioural baseline for subsequent design stages (Domain Modelling,
Architecture, Database Design, and UI/UX Design).

2. Use Case Diagrams
Diagram 1 — Authentication (Generalised User Actor)
This diagram captures the shared authentication use cases owned by the generalised «User» actor.
The Learner and Teacher actors inherit these behaviours via actor generalisation. MoES/NCDC
officials are onboarded administratively and therefore do not use Register Account.
Diagram 2 — Learner
This diagram captures all use cases available to the Learner role, derived from FR-L-03 through
FR-L-11. The Learner inherits authentication behaviour from the generalised «User» actor (shown
in Diagram 1). An «include» dependency between Access Lesson Offline and Download Lesson for
Offline Access reflects the mandatory prerequisite relationship between these two use cases.
--- Page 6 ---
Diagram 3 — Teacher
This diagram captures all use cases available to the Teacher role, derived from FR-T-02 through
FR-T-04, plus analyst additions for material management. The Teacher inherits authentication
behaviour from «User». «include» relationships model the mandatory tagging and sharing steps
within the posting flow. «extend» relationships model the optional edit and remove actions triggered
from viewing posted materials.
--- Page 7 ---
Diagram 4 — MoES/NCDC Official & AI Tutor
This diagram combines the MoES/NCDC Official and AI Tutor actors on a single system boundary.
The MoES/NCDC Official manages curriculum content and national reporting. The AI Tutor is
modelled as a secondary system actor (rectangle) on the right of the boundary, per UML
convention. The «include» and «extend» relationships within the AI Tutor use cases reflect
mandatory knowledge base querying and conditional out-of-scope flagging behaviour.
